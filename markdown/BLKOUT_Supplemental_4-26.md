[1.0] BLKOUT SUPPLEMENTAL

The BLKOUT Supplemental is a reference tool for players.

It includes our matched play optional rules, the latest frequently asked
questions, erratas, clarifications and any relevant rules from expansions for
the matched play experience. The Supplemental does not include Unit cards
or Unit specific Special Rules.

The Supplemental and supporting documents, such as the Universal Special

and rulings.

[2.0] MATCHED PLAY
BLKOUT matched play focuses on increasing the level of strategy and
tactics required to win. It also sets a standard level of gameplay for
players to seek outside of their normal gaming group.

[2.1] MATCHED PLAY RULES
BLKOUT Matched Play includes a section of alternate rules from the core
book. These expand upon the core strategies of BLKOUT and are highly
recommended by the game design team and play testing community.

MATCHED PLAY COVER RULES

Any special rules giving a Model Cover will always count as Partial Cover.
Multiple instances of Partial Cover do not stack to Full Cover.

Full Cover | Shooting at a Model in Cover will only subtract a D10 from
the Shooting Action if the Target is within 1” of the piece of terrain
providing it Cover.

Partial Cover | Models that are in Cover but not within 1” of the terrain
providing them Cover are in Partial Cover. Shooting Actions Targeting
them will neither add nor subtract a D10.

Lean Out | Drawing only line of sight to a Lean Out Marker will give that
Model Partial Cover. They will gain Full Cover if that Model is within 1”
of the piece of terrain blocking Line of Sight to the Model.

MATCHED PLAY STRUCTURES
When using the Matched Play Cover rules, use the following rules instead
of those provided in section [8.4] Terrain of the Core Rulebook.

Structures | Structures are buildings or areas with clear boundaries
that Models might enter into. Discuss with your opponent what terrain
will classify as a structure before your game.

Cover | Models inside a Structure have Full Cover when Targeted by
Models outside the Structure.

• If both the attacking Model and the Target are within the same

Structure, use the normal Matched Play Cover rules to determine
the level of Cover the Models are in.

Line of Sight | Line of Sight may not be drawn through a Structure to
Models or Markers on the other side of a Structure.

CONTROL POINTS
Players will begin each game with 3 Control Points. They may never
regain these Control Points.

Players may spend a Control Point to:

Battle Drills | Spend a Control Point before Activating a Force Unit to
allow it to benefit from a Battle Drill during its Activation. A Unit may
only benefit from one Battle Drill per Activation.

Handler Activation | Once per Round, spend a Control Point to remove
an Activation Marker, and any Ready or Engaged Tokens from a
Handler after it has finished its Activation.

Chained Activation | Once per Round you may spend a Control Point to
Activate a Unit after finishing an Activation with a different Unit.

COUNTER EWAR
When an Enemy Model makes a Data Attack Action, one Model in
your Group with a Data Spike may make a Skill Check. If the Skill Check
results in more successes than the Data Attack Skill Check, the Data
Attack is nullified. This does not count as a Reaction.

DUSTERS
A Player may only use a Duster Unit if both Players agree. If they
agree, any Player may replace one of their Force Units and all of their
Control Points with a single Duster Unit. If both Players take a Duster,
they both keep their Control Points.

HEIGHT
Infantry Model’s whose base is 3” or more above a Target Infantry
Model’s base will treat the Target’s Partial Cover as no Cover, and gain
Partial Cover from any Infantry Model whose base is 3” or more below
their base.

JUKE REACTION
Models can perform the Juke Reaction when Targeted by a Shoot
Action. The Shoot Action rolls -1D10.

If they survive the Shoot Action, they may move up to half of their
Movement Value after the Action concludes.

SMOKE
Models with only part of their base inside Smoke gain Partial Cover and
treat their Targets as in Partial Cover. Models with their entire base
inside Smoke gain Full Cover and treat their Targets as in Full Cover.

Line of Sight may not be drawn through a 3” area around a Smoke
Token to Models or Markers outside of the area around the Token.

HOW TO EARN POINTS
1. Players will earn one point for each enemy Unit Destroyed. At the end of
the Game, Units with more than 50% of their Models Destroyed must roll
a D10. On a 6+, they count as Destroyed.

2. Players will earn 2 points for each quadrant they control at the end of
Rounds 3 and 5.

CONTROLLING A QUADRANT
The Player with more Models fully within a quadrant will have control of it.

Models with a Damage track count as multiple Models equal to their total
Damage tracks. For example, a Model with a Damage track of two counts
as two Models when controlling a quadrant.

HOW TO WIN
The Player with the most points at the end of Round 5 wins.

INFILTRATING
When allowed, a Unit may infiltrate onto the table by beginning their
Activation off the table and using their Reposition movement to move onto
the table, measuring their movement from their Player’s Deployment Edge.

Since the Unit is using their Reposition movement, they are free to take an
Action after their movement, and they can also be Reacted to using the
Overwatch Reaction due to that movement.

SPECIAL RULES
These Special Rules may be chosen by the Players or the event coordinator.

Dome Shields | Models within 4” of a Hardpoint may never have more than
1D10 rolled against them from Shoot Actions and Reactions and do not
suffer Damage from Blast Weapons.

Scrambling | This Hardpoint is treated as a Jammer with an 8” range.

AI and Powered Models may never roll more than 1D10 in a Skill Check
within 8” of a Hardpoint.

Drone Swarms | A Model in Base-to-Base with a Hardpoint may spend
their Action to call in a drone swarm. Remove the Hardpoint after doing
so. Drone Swarm: Place a 25mm marker anywhere on the table. Models
within 2” of the marker must make an Armor Check, suffering 1 Damage
per D10 that fails.

Quadrants will vary in size based on the table size. They will begin 8” from
the edge on a 3x3 ft table and 6” from the edge on a 2x2 ft table.

HOW TO START
1. Players will alternate placing Hardpoints, ensuring they are more than 4
inches from a table edge and another Hardpoint.

2. After setting up Hardpoints, each Player should roll a D10, rerolling
tied results. The Player with the higher roll will then place their Models
within 4 inches of a table edge. Afterwards, the other Player may do so
on the opposite table edge.

Players may choose to keep up to one Unit off of the Table, allowing for it
to Infiltrate later.

3. These table edges serve as the Players’ Deployment Edges and may be
used when Infiltrating.

4. The Game begins, with the Player who placed their Models first having
the Initiative for the first Round.

[4.0] FAQ

[4.1] GENERAL
Does a Unit keep its Armory Item?
No. A Model benefits from the Armory or uses the Weapon only during the
Activation in which it marked its Combat Load. Imagine the Armory as limited
use or one time abilities.

Can I activate more than one Battle Drill on  a Unit?
No. You can only choose one Battle Drill for the Unit to benefit
from during its Activation.

Does the Impisi Force Rule Apply to the whole Force or just the Impisi Insurgents Unit?
The Impisi Force Rule applies to the entire Force, not just the Unit.

[4.2] MOVEMENT
Is there Unit coherency?
No.

Can a Lean Out Move be Reacted to by Overwatch?
Only if the Model is leaning out during a sprint action. The Model is spending its
Movement not Moving that distance when Leaning Out.

When Jumping do I have to have Line of Sight to the final point?
No, as long as Terrain doesn’t prevent the Model from “moving” from its original
point to the new point. IE: Jumping through a wall of a totally enclosed structure.

Can a Unit with Jump (4) jump over an obstacle that is 4 inches high and end up on ground level
on the other side? (Or would they only be allowed to jump up onto the obstacle?)
The Model is allowed to be placed on the other side of the obstacle if there is no
intervening Terrain in the way, such as a roof.

Can a Model come down from a height if they do not have the Jump (X) rule?
No. The Model must find a safer way down, such as stairs or a ladder.

Can a Model climb a surface, or does a Model wishing to move vertically require an obvious
means to do so (e.g., ladder, stack of crates, etc.)?
The Model requires an obvious means to move vertically.

Does the use of an ability or battle drill that gives a Unit/Model “+X” movement increase
the half movement distance a Model can make to avoid Overwatch?
Yes, increase the movement value before determining the half movement value.

[4.3] COMBAT
Can you Ace Armor Checks?
No, you can only Ace Skill Checks.

Do Units with multiple primary weapons (i.e. Peacemakers or Razorbacks) get to fire all of
them as a single Action or just one?
Non-vehicle Models can only use one weapon profile per Shoot action or
Reaction, not all of their listed weapons.

Can you Target additional Models with Auto, if said Models are within your minimum range,
but within 4” of the original Target Model?
No, you cannot Target Models within a weapons minimum range.

How does Seeking work with the matched play Cover rules?
Weapons with the Seeking special rule will only ignore the -1D10 (Shot) penalty
of Models benefiting from Full Cover.

When using a weapon that says it ignores Cover, do I roll 2D10 or 3D10 for the attack?
If the Target is in Cover you will roll 2D10 as you ignore the -1D10 to your Skill
Check of your Target being in Cover, but you do not gain the +1D10 of your
Target being in the open.

How does Auto interact with Cyclic?
When an active Model makes a Shoot action with a weapon that has both Auto
and Cyclic special rules, the sequence of actions goes like;

1.  Make the first attack for the weapon, return fire Reaction is applicable here.

2.  Overwatch Reactions to the Shoot action occur.

3.  The Model makes additional Auto Shoot actions, these cannot be Reacted to.

4.  The Model then chooses to either gain a Ready Token, or repeat steps 1-3.

UN Raid Force Alpha Specialists, if the sniper moves with the deployed weapon, do they still
roll 2D10 from the faction rule?
No, the Deployed USR only allows from 1D10 to be rolled if the Model moved,
thus stopping the Force Rule from triggering.

[4.4] CQC
What is the difference between Melee and CQC?
-Melee is a special rule for certain weapons, and is resolved as a modified
Shoot Action.
-CQC occurs after a Model has completed its action and it is still in Base-to-Base
with an enemy infantry Model and is a skill check roll off [3.3]. CQC continues
until one Model is destroyed.

Does the Drone auto lose CQC or does a CQC not occur?
As Drones cannot participate in CQC, no CQC would occur, even if an enemy
Model is in Base-to-Base contact.

Can Models in CQC range still Shoot?
Yes, Models in CQC can still perform Actions and Reactions.

When does CQC happen?
CQC occurs after the Model has completed its Action and after Overwatch
Reactions have been resolved. So a Model can Reposition into Base-to-Base, be
Reacted to, perform an Action, be Reacted to, and then resolve CQC.

When does CQC occur, after a melee action?
After the Model using the melee weapon has finished its action and any Models
have finished their Reactions.

When does a CQC finish?
When a Model is Destroyed. If both Models survive the initial CQC, roll the
Models’ Skill Checks again (keeping the Damage applied by the previous rolls)
until a Model is Destroyed.

[4.5] EXPLOSIVES AND BLAST
When placing a Blast Token, do I have to see the center of the Token, or do I only need
to see part of it?
You only need to be able to draw line of sight to part of the Blast Token when
placing it.

What special rules apply to Blast attacks? AP and HE versions
-For AP, the following USR’s would apply: AP, Auto, CQB, Cyclic, Deployed, EMP,
Heavy, Indirect, Medium, Seeking, Sustained.
-For HE, the following USR’s would apply: Cyclic, Deployed, EMP,
Heavy, Medium.

Do keywords that provide ‘+1 Shot’ apply to Blast rolls when using them against Infantry?
Eg. Heavy
Yes.

Do Blast Markers require Line of Sight to the Models within their Blast radius?
No, Blast affects all Models within X” of it, regardless of Line of Sight or Cover.
Always agree with your opponent on if this is realistic.

What is the Damage of Blast Weapons?
When Targeting Dusters or Vehicles, use the Damage number printed on the
card for the Weapon. When using Blast [X], it is essentially Damage 1 with the
Target rolling 1D10 for each success of the Skill Check.

Can I place a Blast Marker outside of Line of Sight?
Only if your opponent agrees. We like to eyeball it and say if it’s a thrown weapon
like a hand grenade, then you can simply halve the Range of the Weapon to
simulate the arc.

When you roll the same amount of successes when making a Return Fire Reaction, who wins?
Whichever Model rolled the highest result on a D10 in their Skill Check. If both
Models tie, reroll the Skill Check and any Special Rules.

How does Indirect work with the Blast special rule?
When Targeting Vehicles and Dusters, the Unit Shooting the weapon may use
another Model’s line of sight.

When wanting to attack Infantry Models, the weapon cannot make use of the
Indirect rule, as you are not Targeting a Model.

[4.6] REACTIONS
Can Blast [X] weapons be used in Reactions?
Only against vehicles and Dusters, as Blast Weapons cannot Target
Infantry Models.

Can Blast weapons trigger the Return Fire Reaction?
Yes, as using Blast weapons is still making a Shoot Action.

Does Cyclic work on Reaction Shooting?
No, Cyclic only triggers on a Shoot Action, which can only be done by an
active Unit.

[6.0] UNIVERSAL SPECIAL RULES

AI - This Model may be affected by Special Rules that only affect AI Models.

AP (X) - When a weapon with AP(X) inflicts damage, increase the
Target’s Armor Check by +X for that attack. This makes their armor less
effective—they must roll higher numbers to block damage.

Example: A Model with Armor 2/4 hit by an AP(2) Weapon would now
have Armor 2/6 for that attack.

Auto (X) - After making a Shoot Action with this Weapon, this Model may
make up to X additional Shoot Actions with that Weapon, that cannot be
Reacted to.

Each additional attack must target a different Model within 4” of the
original Target.

Synergies: If the Weapon also has the Cyclic special rule, the Model may
chain multiple Auto(X) attacks, benefiting from the extra Shoot Action
granted by Cyclic.

Limitations: The original Target and all additional Targets must still fall
within the Weapon’s minimum and maximum Range.

Controller - This Unit may activate a friendly Unit after completing its
Activation, without spending a Control Point.

If Controller is granted by a Specialist, the ability is lost when that Model is
removed from play.

CQB - This Weapon may re-roll all failed Shots against a Target within half
of its maximum range.

Example: A Weapon with a 24” Range may re-roll failed Shots against a
Target within 12”.

Cyclic - After making a Shoot Action with this Weapon, this Model may
immediately make one additional Shoot or Ready Action.

The extra Shoot Action is treated the same as any regular Shoot Action,
subject to Weapon’s special rules.

Limitations: Additional Shoot Action must be made with the Weapon that
triggered Cyclic. Cyclic cannot be used in Reactions.

Data Knife - This Model may make a Data Attack Action targeting an AI or
Powered Model within Range. On a successful Skill Check, deal 1 Damage
with no Armor Check allowed.

Data Spike - This Model may make a Data Attack Action to either apply
a Pinned Marker to an enemy Unit or remove a Pinned Marker from a
friendly Unit, on a successful Skill Check.

Deployed - If this Model Moved during its Reposition Step, it rolls only
1D10 when using this Weapon in its Activation.
Reactions are not affected.

Drone - This Model cannot engage in CQC, use ladders, or Lean Out to
gain Line of Sight.

Note: Drones do not participate in CQC — if an enemy Model moves into
Base-to-Base contact, no Close Quarters Combat occurs.

EMP - This Weapon can only Damage Units with the AI Special Rule, and
that Damage ignores Armor Checks.

Heavy - If this Model did not Move during its Reposition Step, it rolls an
extra 1D10 when using this Weapon in its Activation. Reactions with this
Weapon always roll an extra 1D10.

Indirect - This Weapon may Target an enemy Model in Line of Sight to
another Model in its Group, but Range is still measured from the firing Model.

Jammer - Models within range of a Jammer ignore the effects of Pinned
Markers on their Unit, though Models outside the Jammer’s range
remain Pinned.

Lethal - This Model rolls 3D10 for CQC Skill Checks.

Low Tech - This Unit cannot gain Pinned Markers from Data Attacks and
cannot be targeted by Weapons with the Indirect special rule.

Medium - This Weapon may re-roll one failed Shot when used in a
Shoot Action.

Melee - This Weapon ignores Cover penalties. When used in a Shoot
Action, place the attacker Base-to-Base with its Target before resolving
the Action and any Reactions.

Targets may only Return Fire with a Melee Weapon or Juke.

Outsourced - Models in this Unit cannot use Battle Drills, Armory Items,
or the Force Rule.

Note: Some Specialists may have exceptions to this rule — for example,
the E4 Mafia in Hired Killers may explicitly use Battle Drills.

Powered - This Model may be affected by Special Rules that affect
Powered Models.

Reinforcements - At the end of this Unit’s Activation, mark one box to
return a Destroyed Model from this Unit. Place the returned Model
within 4” of another Model from this Unit and out of Line of Sight of all
Enemy Models.

Seeking - This Weapon ignores the -1D10 penalty for shooting a Target
in Cover.

Example: When firing a Seeking Weapon, you roll 2D10 against Targets in
Cover, and 3D10 against Targets not in Cover.

Shield - This Model and one Friendly Infantry Model in Base-to-Base with it
both gain Cover.

Only one Friendly Model may be in Base-to-Base with this Model at a time.

Smoke Grenade | X” - Before this Unit’s Reposition Phase, place a Smoke
Token within X” of one of its Models.

Note: See Rules [8.8] Smoke for more information about this core ability.

Spotter - If at least two Models in this Unit can draw Line of Sight to
the same Enemy Model, Models in this Unit that target that Enemy gain
Medium for that Action.

Sustained (X) - When making an Armor Check against this Weapon, the
Target must re-roll up to (X) successful Armor dice, continuing until that
many have been re-rolled or all rolls fail.

Example: A Model with Armor 1/6 is hit by a Sustained (2) Weapon. It
rolls 1D10 for Armor. If it succeeds, it must reroll that die up to 2 times
or until it fails. The final roll determines the result.

Team Leader - When this Model gains a Ready Token, it may instead give
that token to another Model in the same Unit that has already made an
Action this round.

The receiving Model is treated as if it gained the Ready Token normally.
