ABOL’S
DISCOVERY

The excitement was short-lived. Both Alana
and Oliver tragically perished in a helicopter
crash only a month after the discovery.
The study of the MRB gradually faded from
attention as other crises arose, leaving its
true nature an unresolved cosmic mystery.

By 2030, with the first manned colony
on Mars established, NASA reignited its
interest in ABOL. The Endeavor Mission
deployed thousands of interstellar micro-
probes using NASA’s Orbital Laser
Propulsion Array (OLPA), aimed at reaching
ABOL within a decade. However, decades
would pass before their findings reached
Earth, prompting whispers that humanity
didn’t have time to wait.

In 2032, the United Nations launched the
Trailblazer Missions. Trailblazer 1 sent a
satellite and lander to ABOL to scout for
future manned missions. Three years later,
the multinational Trailblazer 2 mission arrived
to survey ABOL’s surface, a prelude to the
UN’s LEAP colonization efforts. However,
history remembers the Trailblazer 2 crew
for a darker reason—their disappearance.
Though their beacons and landing sites were
discovered when LEAP ships reached orbit
in 2070, the fate of these early explorers
remains an enduring mystery.

A crowd of one thousand will present one
thousand different reasons for leaving
Earth: work, overcrowding, war, life,
children, expectations, death. But the
mainline narrative is much simpler: the
“death” of the planet.

Pollution, overpopulation, destruction,
and dwindling resources dominated the
concerns of the United Nations’ missions
and media broadcasts. Global campaigns
aimed at achieving zero emissions and energy
independence created a race to the bottom
for most nations, drastically disrupting
resource management, food supplies, and
basic human needs.

Initially, Proxima Centauri—the closest
star system to Earth—was disregarded.
Its handful of dead rocks and a gas giant
orbiting the outer edges of the system held
little interest. Early observations from the
Hubble Space Telescope in the late 20th
century caught the eye of astronomers
but went unnoticed by the general public.
That changed in 2022 when the world of
astronomy was rocked by a Massive Radio
Burst (MRB) from this seemingly lifeless
system. Detected by the Very Large Array
(VLA), this mysterious signal captivated a
team at NASA led by astronomers Alana
Black and Oliver Lenwood. A year later, their
work revealed something unexpected: a
previously undiscovered exoplanet within
Proxima Centauri’s habitable zone. The
planet was named ABOL, a combination of
Black and Lenwood’s initials, in their honor.

COLONIZATION
AND DESCENT
INTO MARTIAL
LAW

The early days of ABOL’s colonization were
marked by feverish activity as UN cities
sprouted rapidly. The hulls of colony ships
were repurposed into the framework for
infrastructure, providing steel for skyscrapers
and sprawling developments. With near-
universal employment, few questioned the
UN’s authority during this time. However,
the tide turned after just two years. When
infrastructure reached an “acceptable” state,
the UN halted its development programs,
leading to mass unemployment.

Prefabricated megabuildings meant as
temporary housing became permanent
homes for most, while UN officials and
corporate heads enjoyed lavish headquarters
and military-grade fortifications. Forced into
Federal Contract Programs, the majority
of ABOL’s population found themselves
conscripted into UN service or assigned
undesirable jobs. Those who resisted the
UN’s grip faced grim prospects—white-collar
jobs were monopolized by those under federal
contracts, leaving dissenters to scrape by in
service industries or fall into illicit trades.

In 2073, catastrophic Ash Storms disrupted
UN supply chains, driving many colonists
into the hands of criminal organizations like
Sabra, which provided food and water to
the desperate. Mercenary work flourished,
with groups offering protection for private
farmers and supply convoys, while betting
on these operations became a popular
pastime. The system, dubbed the Killwager,
transformed life-or-death stakes into a
spectator sport, with fortunes made—and
lives lost—over a single raid.

By 2077, tensions boiled over as UN martial
law reached its zenith under the Service,
Settlement, and Security Act (SSSA). Mass
protests erupted in Ethron, ABOL’s largest
city, where armed citizens clashed with UN
forces over control of the space elevator.
When a young girl in a yellow dress was killed
by a warning shot, violence exploded, marking
the beginning of the Battle of Ethron. While
the UN retained nominal control, the rebel
group IRAFEL vowed to break the yoke of UN
authority, igniting a war for ABOL’s future.

LIFE ON ABOL

ABOL, with its hostile environment
and political unrest, is a world of stark
contrasts—a new home for humanity fraught
with danger and mystery. Despite 40 years of
terraforming, it remains a planet of extremes.
Its low-oxygen atmosphere resembles Earth’s
high altitudes, making survival above the
plains without supplemental oxygen perilous.
Most habitation has shifted underground,
where natural caves and craters provide
breathable air and shelter. Water is scarce,
with ABOL’s surface dominated by deserts
and arid savannas. Five massive rivers,
sustained by glaciers and terraforming
efforts, snake across the land, much of their
water disappearing underground into vast
cave systems.

The planet’s climate is volatile, shaped by its
twin moons, Castor and Pollux, which drive its
two seasonal cycles: the milder Castor Phase
and the severe Pollux Phase, during which
superstorms ravage the landscape. These
storms stir up vast amounts of dust and ash,
blocking sunlight for months in some regions
and plunging much of ABOL into darkness.

ABOL’s Southern Hemisphere, particularly the
sprawling Cenriv Crater Complex, serves as
the heart of human settlement. Here, the city
of Ethron stands as a testament to human
ambition, with prefabricated megastructures
stretching across the plains. Further north
lies the Frontier Desert, an unforgiving
wasteland that was once a vast ocean. This
desolate expanse is dotted with outposts, but
rumors persist of a rebel stronghold within
The Thorn, a massive peak towering 17 miles
above the planet’s surface, located at the
center of this harsh desert.

In the Northern Hemisphere lies ABOL
Secundus, a jagged, ash-covered wasteland
where few dare to venture. The Mokhri Pat
mountain range and the corrosive ash from
a supervolcano make this region nearly
uninhabitable, though its rich deposits of
Abolite, a rare energy-dense mineral, continue
to attract dangerous mining operations.
Despite numerous expeditions, the area
remains treacherous, and the discovery of
large Abolite reserves in the desert foothills
may mark the end of human aspirations for
ABOL Secundus.

FLORA &
FAUNA OF
ABOL

ABOL’s flora and fauna reflect the planet’s extreme
conditions, with both native and imported species adapting
to their new environment. The continued evolution of life
on ABOL highlights humanity’s resilience and ingenuity in
facing the challenges of interstellar colonization.

Flora

ABOL’s harsh environment poses significant challenges
for flora. The planet’s vegetation has adapted to
its unique conditions, with some plants exhibiting
remarkable resilience:

CRIMSON FERNS

These plants grow in the lower elevations of the Gran Taźon Mountains. They
are adapted to low-oxygen environments and have developed a reddish hue to
absorb more sunlight during the short, intense periods of daylight.

GLOW MOSS

Found in the subterranean cave systems, Glow Moss emits a bioluminescent glow,
providing light in the dark environments of ABOL’s underground habitats.

TITAN’S CACTUS

Resilient to both high temperatures and low water levels, this cactus is
common in the Grass Sea. Its large, water-storing pads can survive long
periods of drought.

RAZOR GRASS

Dominating its environment, this tall grass is the linchpin of the Grass Sea’s
ecosystem. It gets its name from its razor-sharp blades that can easily draw
blood from uncovered skin.

BONS BUSH

Thick, stout scrub trees found throughout the Sea of Grass. Their wood makes
poor building material due to its almost rubber-like consistency, hence the
nickname “Bons” or Bounce Bushes.

DUNE GRASS

Found in the Frontier Desert, this
hardy plant has deep roots to access
underground water reserves. It can
survive extreme temperatures and is a
vital food source for local herbivores.

vFauna

ABOL’s fauna has evolved to cope with
the planet’s extreme conditions. Some of
the most notable species include:

ASH DRAKES

Small, bat-like reptiles that thrive in the ash-filled environments of Mokhri Pat.
They have developed thick, heat-resistant scales and can survive in the highly
acidic atmosphere.

GLIMMER FISH

These bioluminescent fish are found in ABOL’s underground water systems.
Their glow helps attract prey and provides light in the otherwise dark
aquatic environments.

SAND STRIDERS

Inhabiting the Frontier Desert, these large, insect-like creatures have adapted
to extreme temperatures and scarce water. They are known for their ability to
travel long distances across the arid landscape.

CAVE PYTHONS

These large snakes are found in ABOL’s subterranean environments. They have
adapted to low-oxygen conditions by developing a slower metabolism and a
keen sense of echolocation to navigate dark cave systems, only exerting energy
to strike at potential meals.

POOL SHRIMP

These small crustaceans can be found wherever water pools for long periods,
creating a vast tree of subspecies, including bioluminescent shrimp in ABOL’s cave
systems and even MEGA shrimp that comb the shallows of the Great Rivers.

ASH COBRAS

Among the Earth animal species brought to ABOL were Ash Cobras — massive
snakes evolved from cobras and pit vipers. Exposure to intense radiation
transformed these creature.

GRASS RACERS

Small, snake-like lizards native to the Sea of Grass. These foot-long reptiles use
their limbs and bodies to slide and glide through the massive grass fields, almost
as if they’re swimming.

IDERES RAMS

Native to the Howling Hills surrounding
Hades Point, these large, horned
herbivores are known for their distinctive
vocalizations, described as “human-like
wails.” They have adapted to the low-
oxygen environment and are a key part of
the local ecosystem.

THE
AUTHORITY

Originally the United Nations ABOL,
the Authority evolved into a socialized
oligarchy by 2085. Formed amid global
crises in 2029, the Republic of United
Nations united countries like Germany,
France, and Italy to stabilize Europe,
eventually becoming a global superpower.

The Authority faced challenges from falling
debris, ash storms, and insurgent groups
like the Impisi and Sabra Cartel. Attempts
to control resources led to the Service,
Settlement, and Security Act (SSSA) of
2074, igniting the “Yellow Revolution” and
a major firefight in Ethron.

In 2030, the US discovered ABOL and
proposed the LEAP (Leaving Earth
Program) to the UN, launching the
“Hope” PR campaign for colonization.
The LEAP Act, passed in 2032, allowed
the UN and the Pan Arab Oil Corporation
(PAOC) to land on ABOL with a two-year
buffer for follow-up ships, while others
needed UN approval.

Upon arrival in 2070, the UN fleet,
led by the flagship Hope, aggressively
enforced the LEAP Act, leading to
conflicts with Russian vessels. The UN
and PAOC established the capital city of
Ethron in the Cenriv I crater. However,
in 2072, the UN betrayed other
national ships by launching a nuclear
strike in space, leaving fewer than
100,000 survivors on ABOL.

In response to rising insurgency, the
UN and PAOC’s Ibragim pursued
counterinsurgency efforts. The arrival
of the Second Wave in 2080 sparked
the Second Landing War, allowing
the UN to seize key cities like New
Damascus and San Christo.

By 2085, the Authority of Humanity was
established under the Great Authority
Act, promising unity and galaxy-wide
colonization at the cost of extreme
government control. The Authority
maintains a tense peace with the
Headless and focuses on expanding its
military capabilities.

THE TWIN
MOONS
PROGRAM

The Twin Moons Program, a UN super-soldier initiative, developed
enhanced soldiers known as Urban Tactical Groups (UTGs). These elite
units operate in pairs, specializing in high-risk missions and intelligence
operations, overseen by Authority Intelligence Agency (AIA) Director David
Hackett. Inspired by the 1990s “Dolly” sheep cloning research, UTGs are
formed from clones trained together, consisting of “Athena” (mothers) as
officers and “Artemis” (daughters) as NCOs.

UTGs are capable of self-replication, allowing for force expansion over
time. Initially led by UTG-003 “Hera,” the first successful UTG soldier fit for
combat, they play a key role in Authority propaganda and recruitment.

UN RAID
FORCE ALPHA

UN Raid Force Alpha represents the pinnacle of military prowess on
ABOL. These elite female warriors, trained from birth to be lethal, work
in pairs for synchronized battlefield support. Enhanced by gene therapy
and cutting-edge technology, they are more experienced than any three
mercenaries combined. Supported by ‘Golem’ Combat Units — drones
with the consciousness and experience of fallen combatants—this team is
the Authority’s most formidable force.

ORK

Rozwiązania Konfliktów Orłów (ORK), the military

CHIMERA

Chimera emerged from extremist factions of the Ibragim civil war,
seeking refuge with Manticor. These zealots, who advocate for a fusion
of technology and biology, integrated advanced tech and enhanced
musculature with Manticor’s strength. Their technology significantly
boosted Manticor’s capabilities, allowing for efficient bionic augmentations.

Chimera members often replace damaged limbs with mechanical
substitutes crafted in Ibragim’s labs. The most heavily augmented
members, known as Disciples, frequently operate advanced equipment
like Cyka suites, finding synchronization easier due to their enhancements.

MANTICOR
BORZ

Manticor Borz acts as the formidable right arm of

BANAK

Banak Strategic, founded by Hakob Dharbinyan in 2084, became a

Following the PAOC’s collapse, Banak found itself in conflict with The
Authority after rumors surfaced that a SOC-SAR team recovered old-
world technology from a quarantine zone.

By 2073, ash storms severed UN supply

WAR ON
ABOL

In the future, warfare has shifted from sprawling conflicts to precise, high-stakes
skirmishes. Massive armies are relics of the past, replaced by compact, specialized
units executing missions with pinpoint accuracy. The battlefield is now dominated by
artificial intelligence combat units that seamlessly integrate with human operatives,
adjusting tactics in real time to the fluid nature of modern combat. Skies are filled
with swarms of micro drones conducting reconnaissance and strike missions with
minimal collateral damage. Warfare has become a game of chess, where a single
move can alter the course of an entire conflict.

INORGANICS

Inorganic combat units have revolutionized the battlefield. Constructed from
advanced alloys and driven by sophisticated programming, these units operate
without human limitations such as fatigue or fear.. They excel in reconnaissance and
covert operations, adapting to harsh environments with ease. Equipped with adaptive
camouflage and silent operational modes, they can infiltrate enemy lines, relay critical
data, and execute precision strikes.

Some units are engineered for heavy assault, wielding weaponry that
surpasses human capacity, while others focus on rapid, agile engagements.
Integrated with cutting-edge sensors and AI-driven decision matrices, these
units coordinate with minimal human input, ensuring tactical superiority and
significantly reducing casualties.

DUSTERS

The Mobile Infantry Direct Action System (MIDAS), commonly known as a “Duster,”
is a game-changer on the battlefield. Developed by major defense contractors like
General Dynamics, Raytheon Technologies, and Lockheed Martin, Dusters were
designed to enhance urban warfare capabilities, providing exceptional agility and
firepower in complex environments.

After the Second Landing, military contractors sold blueprints and schematics
to the highest bidders, leading to the widespread adoption of Dusters across
various factions. Their versatility and formidable presence have transformed urban
combat, reshaping the dynamics of battles on ABOL.
